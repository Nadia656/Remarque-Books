Erich Maria Remarque

Erich Maria Remarque was a German writer. He started writing his first novel “The Dream Room” at the age of 16. During World War I, he was wounded by shrapnel, and sent to an army hospital in Germany where he spent the rest of the war. He then held various jobs including teacher, librarian, businessman, journalist and editor. Remarque's anti-war themes led to his condemnation by Nazi propaganda minister Joseph Goebbels as "unpatriotic". He was able to use his literary success to relocate to Switzerland and the United States, where he became a naturalized citizen.
